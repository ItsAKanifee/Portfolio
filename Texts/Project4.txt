TL;DR: CMOS 4-bit DAC design, layout, and verification achieving 0–1.0 V range with fabrication-ready verification.
Tech: CMOS, SPICE, Cadence, LVS, DRC
Role: Design & Verification
Impact: 0–1.0 V output range, ~0.148 V resolution, 16 steps (4-bit)

## 📌 Project Overview

### Problem

Design a functional DAC capable of converting digital inputs into accurate analog voltage outputs while maintaining stability and manufacturability.

---

### What I Did

- Designed logic circuits using **inverters and transmission gates** to control signal propagation

- Implemented **latch and counter functionality** for sequential bit handling

- Built and simulated a **multi-stage DAC** to generate analog output signals

- Analyzed **voltage outputs and resolution**, including step size and output combinations

- Investigated **impedance effects (≈2.64 kΩ)** and their impact on output range

- Completed **full physical layout** and validated design with:

✅ DRC (design rule check)

- ✅ LVS (layout vs schematic matching)

---

## 📊 Impact & Results

- Achieved DAC output range of **0–1.0 V** with discrete voltage steps

- Identified minimum voltage resolution of **~0.148 V**

- Verified **16 unique voltage combinations (4-bit DAC behavior)**

- Demonstrated how impedance influences analog output accuracy

- Delivered **fabrication-ready layout** with clean verification results

---

## 🧠 Lessons Learned

- Small design factors (like impedance) can significantly affect analog accuracy

- Synchronization between digital components is critical for meaningful output

- Layout verification (DRC/LVS) is as important as circuit functionality

- Bridging digital logic and analog behavior requires careful tradeoffs
